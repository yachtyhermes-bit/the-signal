## STORY 3: ORACLE'S $40 BILLION HANGOVER

**Hook:** Oracle just beat earnings expectations and raised guidance. The stock crashed 10%. Welcome to the ugly side of the AI infrastructure arms race.

**The Narrative:**
- Oracle reported Q4 results Wednesday after the bell: beat on top and bottom line. Revenue up 21% YoY. Cloud revenue up 47%. Raised EPS guidance to $8.05.
- Stock dropped 10% in extended trading.
- Why? The company announced plans to raise $40 billion more through debt and equity — including a $20 billion share sale — to finance AI data center buildout.
- This is ON TOP of $48 billion already raised in fiscal 2026 ($43B debt, $5B equity).
- Free cash flow for the fiscal year: NEGATIVE $23.7 billion.
- Capex jumped 162% to $55.7 billion. Depreciation nearly doubled.
- Remaining performance obligations: $638 billion (up 363% YoY) — so the pipeline is massive. But the market is asking: at what cost?
- Cloud infrastructure revenue hit $5.8B (up 93%), but still dwarfed by AWS's $37.6B.
- The core tension: AI demand is real and growing — but the spend to capture it is astronomical, dilutive, and debt-heavy.
- This is the flip side of the SpaceX mania. Everyone's chasing the next thing, but nobody wants to pay the bill.

**Why It's Perfect for The Signal:**
- Classic "beat and get crushed" narrative
- Negative $23.7B FCF is a jaw-dropping number
- Perfect counter-programming to AI hype
- Dilution fear angle resonates with retail investors
- The "AI buildout is a money furnace" thesis in one stock
