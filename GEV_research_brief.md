## BULL CASE
- Global power supercycle: AI/data center build, manufacturing reshoring, electrification all driving unprecedented electricity demand
- Supply-constrained gas turbine market → 300% price increases, massive pricing power
- Electrification backlog ($39B equipment alone) provides multi-year revenue visibility
- Margins expanding rapidly: 390 bps EBITDA improvement in Q1; 500 bps in Power; 590 bps in Electrification
- Net cash balance sheet ($9.77B cash vs $3.76B debt)
- Duopoly position with Siemens Energy; essentially no new competitors in large gas turbines
- Analyst consensus: 16-18% upside to mean/median targets

## BEAR CASE / RISKS
- Valuation: P/B 20x, P/TTM-Revenue 7x, EV/EBITDA 81x — rich for an industrial
- EPS volatility: massive one-time items in Q4'25/Q1'26 mask true run-rate profitability
- Wind segment remains a drag (offshore wind market still recovering, legacy loss-making contracts)
- High ROE (75.7%) partly reflects leveraged equity base ($11.2B equity on $63B assets)
- Tariff/trade risk on global supply chain
- Stock up 100%+ in 12 months → mean reversion risk; already pricing in much of the supercycle
- Earnings concentration in Power; if AI capex cycle slows, growth decelerates
