## 8. EUV vs DUV MARKET SHARE

### EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet) Lithography
- **ASML has 100% market share** in EUV lithography — the only producer in the world
- EUV system sales represented ~**48% of FY2025 net system sales**
- Each EUV machine costs **$350-380 million** (standard) to **$400+ million** (High-NA)
- Production capacity: ~40-50 EUV machines per year (2024 baseline)
- EUV machine lifespan: 10-15 years with continuous upgrades
- EUV required for chips at **5nm and below** — for advanced chips, ASML has 100% monopoly

### DUV (Deep Ultraviolet) Lithography
- ASML has **~80-90% share** of the overall lithography market (EUV + DUV combined)
- DUV systems used for mature/legacy nodes and some advanced nodes (7nm with multi-patterning)
- DUV is now threatened by expanding Chinese export restrictions

### EUV Market Size
- Global EUV lithography market: ~$12.2B in 2025, projected to reach $25.1B by 2034
- EUV market growing at 9.35% CAGR (to $40.5B by 2031)

**Sources: DDG search results [items 2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9 from search #1]; Wikipedia ASML Holding page**

## 9. HIGH-NA EUV STATUS

- **Next-generation High Numerical Aperture (High-NA) EUV** systems increase resolution for sub-2nm nodes
- First High-NA EUV system (TWINSCAN EXE:5000) **shipped to Intel in December 2023** for R&D qualification
- **TSMC received first High-NA EUV machine in Q4 2024**
- High-NA machines increase the numerical aperture from 0.33 to 0.55
- Price: **$350-400+ million per machine** (vs ~$200M for latest standard EUV)
- ASML's record Q1 2026 backlog includes 45 EUV units (including High-NA demand)
- High-NA opens the "sub-2nm era" of chip manufacturing
- TSMC's planned $52-56B 2026 capex flows directly to ASML for EUV/High-NA tooling

**Sources: Wikipedia (High-NA section); DDG search results [items 5, 6, 9]; Data Center Dynamics (2024)**

## 11. EXPORT RESTRICTIONS AND REGULATORY RISK

### Timeline of restrictions
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Sep 2019 | Netherlands requires export licenses for EUV shipments to China |
| Sep 2023 | Dutch government places restrictions on certain DUV chip equipment exports to China |
| Jan 2024 | Dutch government placed further restrictions on advanced chipmaking equipment shipments to China |
| Sep 2024 | Netherlands aligned DUV policy with US restrictions; retakes export control from US jurisdiction |
| Jan 2025 | Tightened export controls again — ASML must apply for Dutch (not US) licenses |
| 2025-2026 | Netherlands requires export licenses for ASML 1970i/1980i DUV tool servicing in China |
| Apr 2026 | US Commerce Secretary Lutnick alleged ASML EUV machines may have reached China (ASML denied). Stock dropped 2.7% on the news. |

### Current Risk: The MATCH Act
- US lawmakers proposed the **MATCH Act** targeting ASML's DUV lithography machines to China
- Would restrict immersion DUV lithography (ASML's strongest market segment) in China
- If enacted, could significantly impact the ~33% of ASML's revenue from China

### Key points
- ASML has NEVER been permitted to export EUV machines to China
- China accounts for 33% of ASML's EUR 32.7B FY2025 revenue (mostly DUV sales)
- Chinese competitor SMEE has a working DUV prototype — if shipping by 2027, US export controls would be undermined
- ASML denies that any EUV machines have reached China
- The real structural risk is DUV restrictions (proposed MATCH Act)

**Sources: Wikipedia (extensive export control section); DDG search results [all items from search #3]; Reuters, CNN, AP News reporting**
