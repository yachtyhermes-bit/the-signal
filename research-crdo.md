**Business Summary:** Credo designs high-speed connectivity chips and systems that move data inside AI data centers. The company's products — active electrical cables (AECs), optical DSPs, retimers, and SerDes IP — sit between GPUs, switches, and servers, enabling reliable data transfer at speeds up to 1.6T.

---

## 2. WHAT CREDO ACTUALLY MAKES (Simplified)

Credo's product portfolio can be understood in four layers:

1. **Active Electrical Cables (AECs)** — Copper cables with intelligent chips built into the connectors. These are Credo's flagship product (famous "purple cables"). They replace fiber optic cables for short-reach AI cluster connections because they're cheaper, use less power, and are more reliable. Market share: ~73% of AEC market.

2. **Optical DSPs (Digital Signal Processors)** — Chips that clean up and boost optical signals traveling over fiber. Credo's "Robin" 800G DSP and "Cardinal" 1.6T DSP families target next-gen AI networks.

3. **Retimers** — Chips that recondition and re-time high-speed electrical signals so they can travel farther across circuit boards and cables without degrading. Credo makes them for PCIe 6/7 and Ethernet standards.

4. **SerDes IP** — Certifiable intellectual property blocks (serializer/deserializer cores) that Credo licenses to other chip companies. This was Credo's original business. Its SerDes technology is considered best-in-class for power efficiency.

5. **ZeroFlap Optical Transceivers** — New product line that predicts and prevents network link flaps (dropped connections) — a major problem in AI training clusters where a single flap can waste thousands of GPU-hours.

**Announced:** April 13, 2026
**Closed:** May 28, 2026
**Deal Value:** ~$1.3B ($750M cash + ~$123M in shares + up to ~$430M earnout based on milestones)

**What It Adds:** Silicon photonics photonic integrated circuit (SiPh PIC) technology — enables 800G, 1.6T, and 3.2T optical connectivity modules
**Strategic Rationale:** Creates a vertically integrated optical stack: Credo's SerDes + Credo's DSPs + DustPhotonics' silicon photonics = end-to-end optical interconnect capability
**Revenue Expectation:** Combined optical portfolio expected to generate $500M–$600M+ in FY2027

## 7. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

| Competitor | Overlap | Credo's Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Broadcom** (AVGO) | Switch ASICs, custom AI chips, some optical | Credo is more focused, AEC-first, nimbler in connectivity |
| **Marvell** (MRVL) | Optical DSPs, data center networking, custom silicon | Credo founded by ex-Marvell engineers; Credo has AEC market leadership |
| **Astera Labs** (ALAB) | PCIe retimers, data center connectivity | Credo has broader portfolio (AEC + optical + SerDes IP) |
| **Nvidia** (NVDA) | NVLink, InfiniBand — but different connectivity layer | Credo is complementary, not directly competitive |
| **Coherent / Lumentum** | Optical components | Credo competes more at system/cable level |

**Key competitive insight:** Credo is not trying to beat Broadcom/Marvell at switches or custom ASICs — it wins on the *connectivity fabric* between the compute nodes. The AEC market is Credo's to lose (~73% share per analysts). Competition with Marvell is heating up in optical DSPs, which is where the DustPhotonics acquisition becomes critical.

## 11. BULL THESIS (2-3 sentences)

Credo is the quintessential "picks and shovels" AI play — every AI cluster, regardless of which company builds the GPU or designs the switch, needs Credo's connectivity chips to move data reliably between thousands of processors. With revenue tripling to $1.34B, gross margins near 68%, a virtually debt-free balance sheet, and an 80%+ growth guide for FY2027, the company is executing flawlessly. The DustPhotonics acquisition extends Credo's moat from copper cables into optical — doubling its addressable market just as hyperscalers begin the largest data center buildout in history.

---

## 12. BEAR CASE / RISKS (1-2 sentences)

Customer concentration is extreme — the top two customers account for 71% of revenue (39% and 32%), and losing either would be devastating. At ~107x trailing earnings and 35x revenue, the stock prices in years of perfection, leaving zero room for execution missteps, a slowdown in AI capex, or intensifying competition from Marvell and Broadcom in optical interconnects.
