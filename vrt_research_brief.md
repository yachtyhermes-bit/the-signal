## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Vertiv Holdings Co (NYSE: VRT) designs, manufactures, and services critical digital infrastructure technologies — power management, thermal/cooling management, and IT infrastructure solutions — for data centers, communication networks, and commercial/industrial environments. Headquartered in the US, 34,000 employees. Sector: Industrials (Electrical Equipment & Parts). Vertiv IPO'd in 2018 and joined the S&P 500 in March 2026.

**The thesis:** Vertiv is the purest picks-and-shovels play on the AI data center boom. Every new data center needs power distribution units (PDUs), uninterruptible power supplies (UPS), liquid and air cooling systems, and prefabricated modular infrastructure — all Vertiv's wheelhouse. As hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet) race to build AI-optimized facilities, Vertiv's backlog and revenue are exploding.

## 5. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

- **Positioning:** Broadly diversified power management across electrical, aerospace, and grid infrastructure.
- **Strengths:** Dividends since 1923, low beta (1.18), diversified revenue.
- **Weaknesses vs VRT:** Slower revenue growth (16.8% vs VRT's 30.1%), lower ROE (20.8% vs 45.1%), higher leverage (D/E 110%).
- **Valuation:** 39.16x P/E — cheaper than VRT, but lower growth justifies the premium.

### Schneider Electric — ~€130B+ market cap (European-listed)
- Global leader in energy management. Major data center power business.
- Strengths: EcoStruxure platform, end-to-end solutions.
- Less data-center-pure than VRT; European listing limits US accessibility.

### nVent Electric (NVT) — $24.9B market cap
- Electrical connection/protection + some data center thermal.
- Strengths: High growth (53.5%), lower absolute valuation.
- Weaknesses: Much smaller ($4.3B revenue), lower margins, far less FCF ($210M vs VRT's $1.96B).

### Key competitive dynamics:
- **Vertiv, Eaton, and Schneider are Nvidia's three "power systems" partners** for 800 VDC architecture (per BofA). Vertiv will ship 800 VDC sidecar in 2027.
- **Vertiv is the most concentrated pure-play** — more levered to data center demand than any peer.
- **Backlog doubled to $15B+** signals market share gains.
- **Liquid cooling is the battleground** — CoolIt acquisition positions Vertiv well.

## 6. MACRO CONTEXT — AI DATA CENTER BUILD-OUT

### The demand picture (Goldman Sachs Research):
- Global data center power demand forecast to increase **50% by 2027** and **165% by 2030** vs 2023 baseline of 55 GW.
- Data center capacity expected to reach ~122 GW by 2030.
- An estimated **$720 billion in grid spending through 2030** needed.
- AI workload share of data center power: 14% in 2023 to projected 27% by 2027.

### Cooling is the hidden story:
- Roughly **35-40% of a hyperscaler's energy consumption is from cooling** (Goldman Sachs).
- Rack densities rising fast — driving demand for **liquid cooling** (Vertiv's CoolIt).
- Europe data center PDU/PSU market: $2.45B (2024) to **$20.05B by 2035** (CAGR 21.23%).

### Infrastructure bottlenecks as tailwinds:
- Data center vacancy rates at historic lows; construction pre-leased.
- Grid interconnection delays favor **prefabricated modular solutions** — Vertiv's specialty.
- Q1 2026 call highlighted: "speed-to-deployment, high-density power architecture, liquid cooling, prefabrication, grid constraints, and lifecycle services" as demand drivers.

### Macro risks:
- **Tariffs** on steel/aluminum/Chinese components; Vertiv managing via price-cost mitigation.
- **800 VDC risk** — could reduce "content per MW"; Bernstein says adoption unlikely before 2028.
- **AI capex cyclicality** — concentrated exposure is a double-edged sword.
- **Valuation risk** — 79x trailing earnings leaves no room for error.
