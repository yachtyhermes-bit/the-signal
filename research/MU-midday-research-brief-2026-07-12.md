## 2. $250B U.S. INVESTMENT BOOST (announced July 9, 2026)

**What's New:**
- Micron raised planned U.S. investment to **more than $250 billion through 2035**
- This is a **$50B increase** from the $200B commitment announced June 2025
- Cumulative increase of **$80B** from the original $170B baseline

**Key Details from Micron Press Release & Reuters:**
- **First concrete poured** at Clay, New York megafab site — **over one quarter ahead of schedule**
- Goal to produce **40% of DRAM in the U.S.**
- **Up to 4 fabs** planned at Clay site — largest semiconductor manufacturing site in U.S. history
- **90,000+ total U.S. jobs** expected; 50,000 in New York (9,000 direct Micron)
- **$3B** dedicated to strengthening the U.S. semiconductor supply chain ecosystem
- $500M of that funds **GlobalWafers' 300mm raw silicon wafer facility** in Sherman, Texas + 10-year supply agreement

**Construction Partners:** Bechtel (lead), Jacobs (architecture), Gilbane (preconstruction)
**First Wafer Output:** Idaho first fab mid-calendar 2027; second fab late calendar 2028
**1α DRAM:** Already in initial production at Manassas, VA facility

**Political Angle:** Trump administration touted as "Trump effect"; Secretary Lutnick, Gov. Hochul at ceremony
**CHIPS Act:** $6.1B CHIPS grant + billions in Investment Tax Credit secured (per Sen. Schumer)

## 6. AI MEMORY DEMAND THESIS

### HBM Supply Crunch
- Entire HBM sector sold out through 2026
- Memory shortage expected to persist into **2030** (new fabs take years to build)
- Data center revenue run rate: **>$100B annualized** for Micron alone

### Long-Term Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs)
- **16 SCAs** signed (3-5 year terms) across data center, auto, consumer
- **$22B** in customer financial commitments
- **40% of revenue** from long-term contracts with minimum-price built in
- **Take-or-pay structure** — cannot be canceled (per Sumit Sadana on earnings call)
- Prior memory agreements lasted just 1 year; these run 5 years

### Data Center Buildout
- Hyperscaler capex continues accelerating (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta)
- Nvidia Rubin platform driving next wave of HBM demand
- Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, and other AI labs competing for compute

### Structural Change Argument
> "Memory industry has historically been boom-bust cyclical. SCAs, supply discipline, and capex constraints may have structurally extended the upcycle."

## 7. RISK FACTORS

### 1. Michael Burry Short (July 1, 2026)
- Entered short position at **$1,051.87/share**
- Called rally "fear of missing out, greater fool theory, public commitment bias"
- Famously said: **"Micron defines cyclical like no other"** — noted 34 drawdowns >30% in 42 years
- Also shorted Nvidia, Tesla, Applied Materials, SOXX ETF
- Stock has since declined ~7% from his entry (~$979 now)

### 2. Cyclical Memory Risk
- Historically, memory stocks have brutal downcycles (negative gross margins as recently as 2023)
- Patrick Moorhead: *"A few years back these memory makers were negative gross margins... selling stuff below costs"*
- 34 drawdowns of 30%+ in 42 years is the data point Burry highlighted

### 3. Valuation & Sentiment Risks
- Stock up **~700% over past year** — extreme move
- Forward P/E of 6.54x looks cheap, but trailing P/E of 22x and the cyclicality question loom
- The Motley Fool: "Micron doesn't look expensive on today's numbers... but the question is sustainability"

### 4. Competition
- SK Hynix raising $26.5B to expand capacity — could lead to oversupply
- Samsung aggressively pursuing HBM4 share
- Chinese competitors potentially entering market (noted by BofA)

### 5. Pricing Headwinds
- BofA flagged elevated memory pricing as potential "tax" on data center capex
- Demand destruction risk in price-sensitive markets (mobile, auto)
- Potential for larger-than-expected ASP declines

### 6. Hedge Fund Selling
- Burry's short position sharpens focus on institutional positioning
- Stock down ~10% in last month from highs
- Broader tech sell-off in late June/early July
