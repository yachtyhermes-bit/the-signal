## PROGRAMS & CONTRACTS (Source: prior factcheck + news, verified)

### Drone/UAV Programs:
- **XQ-58A Valkyrie**: Low-cost attritable stealth combat drone for USAF. Key platform for the Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCA) program. CCA calls for 1,000+ autonomous aircraft (verified via DoD sources in prior factcheck).
- **BQM-177A (formerly BQM-167)**: Supersonic target drone for Navy/Air Force training. **$400M+ in contracts** (plausible per prior factcheck; program confirmed as significant revenue driver).
- **Elroy Air Partnership** (announced Jun 26, 2026): Exclusive U.S. manufacturer for Elroy Air autonomous cargo drones.

### Competitive Analysis:
- **vs L3Harris**: LHX is 6x larger, much more profitable (9.7% vs 1.8% operating margin), but growing slowly (1.9%). KTOS is the high-growth disruptor with 22.6% revenue growth. LHX does communications, EW, aerospace — less exposure to autonomous systems.
- **vs Northrop Grumman**: NOC dominates in stealth bombers, ICBMs, and space. Strong margins (11.7%), low P/E (15.6x). KTOS competes in the adjacent unmanned/collaborative combat space where NOC is also a CCA contender (B-21 Raider team).
- **vs AeroVironment**: AVAV is the closest pure-play peer (switchblade drones, PUMA). Both are small-cap defense tech plays. AVAV has larger revenue ($1.6B vs $1.4B) but negative margins (operating margin -5.1%). KTOS has better profitability and faster growth. AVAV trades at similar forward P/E (~36x).
