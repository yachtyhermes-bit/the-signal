## 3. COMPETITIVE POSITION vs SpaceX

### Verified Facts:
- **Electron** is the only regularly-flying small launch vehicle in the US not owned by SpaceX (PBS News, Dec 30, 2025)
- Rocket Lab has "carved out a near-monopoly in launching small satellites" (PBS News)
- Rocket Lab: 13,000 employees vs SpaceX. Rocket Lab market cap ~$53B vs SpaceX ~$800B valuation (PBS)
- Rocket Lab revenue growth (63.5% YoY in Q1 2026) exceeds SpaceX's ~50% growth rate from 2023→2024 (Reddit/data analysis)
- Rocket Lab completed **21 Electron launches in 2025** with **100% mission success rate** (Yahoo Finance, parameter.io)
- Rocket Lab's Neutron is priced at **$50-55M/launch** vs SpaceX Falcon 9 at ~$67M (TradingKey)

### SpaceX IPO Impact (June 2026):
- SpaceX (ticker: SPCX) completed its IPO in mid-June 2026
- RKLB stock fell ~11% on SpaceX IPO debut (June 12) as investors rotated capital (Barchart)
- RKLB hit all-time high of $151 prior to pullback (Investing.com)
- Despite short-term selling pressure, analysts view SpaceX IPO as net positive for sector visibility (Seeking Alpha, Motley Fool)

### Key Competitive Advantages:
1. Only US-based commercial launch provider besides SpaceX with demonstrated reliability (100% in 2025)
2. Vertically integrated: makes own solar panels, reaction wheels, software, spacecraft buses
3. Two launch sites (New Zealand + Virginia for Electron; Virginia for Neutron)
4. Defense contracts (NSSL) — SpaceX already dominates this market with Falcon Heavy

## 4. NEUTRON ROCKET STATUS

### Timeline (verified):
| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| March 1, 2021 | Neutron announced | Wikipedia |
| July 2024 | Archimedes engine hot fire test | ACMA |
| Feb 2025 | Successful Archimedes hot fire, targeting mid-2025 first launch | ACMA, Feb 5 2025 |
| Aug/Sep 2025 | Launch Complex 3 (Wallops) officially opened | CompositesWorld, NASA Spaceflight |
| Late 2025 | Qualification and acceptance testing completed on composite structures | NASASpaceFlight, Jan 2026 |
| Jan 2026 | Stage 1 tank ruptured during pressure testing (composite manufacturing defect) | Space.com, The Register |
| Feb 27, 2026 | First launch postponed to **Q4 2026** (late 2026) at earliest | SpaceNews, Aviation Week |
| Q4 2026 | Target for first Neutron flight (current plan) | Multiple sources |

### Neutron Specifications:
- **Payload:** 13,000 kg to LEO (partially reusable configuration)
- **Stage 1:** Reusable, 22.9 ft (7m) diameter, carbon composite structure
- **Stage 2:** 16.4 ft (5m) diameter, carbon composite, contained within Hungry Hippo fairing
- **Engines:** Archimedes (methalox)
- **Landing:** Sea landing on "Return on Investment" barge
- **Launch site:** LC-3, Wallops Flight Facility, Virginia

### Development Cost:
- Rocket Lab spent $250-300M from IPO proceeds on Neutron development (Wikipedia)
- R&D expense FY2025: $270.7M (up from $174.4M in FY2024) — includes Neutron + other programs

### Key Issue:
- Carbon composite tank manufacturing defect caused failure during pressure test
- Company plans to switch to automatic fiber laying machine for production (eliminates manual defects)
- Tank design being modified for increased safety margin

## 8. INVESTMENT THESIS SUMMARY

### Bull Case:
- Revenue growth 38-64% annually with improving margins
- Record $2.2B backlog provides visibility
- Space Systems now dominant segment (67% of revenue, 74% of backlog)
- Neutron (if successful in Q4 2026) opens $14B+ medium-launch market
- Only US competitor to SpaceX in vertical integration
- $5.6B NSSL contract access validates defense credentials
- >$2B liquidity position — no near-term funding risk
- SpaceX IPO raising sector-wide visibility

### Bear Case:
- Still deeply unprofitable (-$198M net loss in FY2025)
- Extreme valuation: 66.9x EV/Revenue for a company losing money
- Neutron delayed, carbon composite tanks have had failures
- SpaceX IPO creates direct public comp with much larger scale
- Stock -44% from all-time high ($151 → $84.54)
- Neutron capex will keep FCF negative through first flights
