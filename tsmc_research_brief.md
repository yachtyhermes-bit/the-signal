**"TSMC's AI Engine Just Hit Escape Velocity"**

Focus on the new financials (Q1 2026 beat, margins expanding, $52-56B capex) and the 2nm ramp as catalysts that separate this moment from the "Blueprint" piece published in May. Key hooks:

1. **Gross margin jumped to 66.2%** — up 400bps from Q4 2025 — as 3nm yields mature and pricing power holds
2. **2nm first-year output 45% higher than 3nm** — N2 is ramping faster than any prior node
3. **Capex wall as competitive moat** — $56B is more than Intel's entire market cap. Samsung can't match
4. **Arizona is no longer aspirational** — first fab profit-making, second fab getting equipment, $20B fresh injection
5. **Geopolitical overlay** — US tariff deal (20%→15%), but Taiwan minister says US won't match Taiwan capacity

**To differentiate from "Blueprint" (May 28):** That was a broad monopoly thesis. This is a *momentum check* — showing the flywheel accelerating through concrete numbers: higher margins, faster node ramps, bigger capex, and real US fab output.
